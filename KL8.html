THRISSUR - Cultural Capital Of Kerala

Also known as the cultural capital of Kerala, Thrissur is a city, globally renowned for the popular and magnificent Pooram Festival. From ancient times, Thrissur with its cultural heritage and archaeological wealth has played a significant role in the political history of Kerala as well as South India.Thrissur as a cultural hub is home to the the prestigious Sahitya and Sangeetha Nataka Academies of Kerala and also Kerala Kalamandalam. Apart from its significantly rich past, Thrissur has a vibrant and delightful present.

MAJOR ATTRACTIONS

Athirapilly Waterfalls

Situated at a height of 80 feet near the Sholayar Ranges, the majestic Athirapally Falls are known as the Niagara Falls of India. The river flows all the way through the Vazhachal Forest toward the Arabian Sea. The visual of the mighty Sholayar Peaks as its backdrop is a commanding site. It is also a great place for Bird lovers as it is the only place where four endangered Hornbill species are seen.

Kerala's first mosque - Cheraman Juma Masjid and first christian church - St. Thomas Church, Palayoor is in Thrissur.

Raja Rama Varma popularly known as 'Sakthan Thampuran' is the architect of the present Thrissur town.

Vadakkunnathan Temple

Vadakkumnathan Temple is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva at heart of Thrissur. This temple is a classical example of the architectural style of Kerala. According to popular local lore, this is the first temple built by Parasurama, the sixth avatara of Vishnu. Thekkinkadu Maidan, encircling the Vadakkumnathan Temple, is the main venue of the renowned Thrissur Pooram festival.

Kerala Kalamandalam

Kerala Kalamandalam is a major center for learning Indian performing arts, especially those that developed in the Southern states of India, with the special emphasis on Kerala. It is situated in the small town of Cheruthuruthy on the banks of the Bharathapuzha river
