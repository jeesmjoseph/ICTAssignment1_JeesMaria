THIRUVANANTHAPURAM - State Capital Of Kerala

Thiruvananthapuram with its many temples and museums has a persona of real elegance. A long shoreline, with internationally renowned beaches, historical monuments, backwater stretches, and a rich cultural heritage make this district a much sought after tourist destination. The wooded highlands on the Western Ghats give some of the most enchanting picnic spots in the city. With good infrastructure, the city is an excellent base to explore Varkala, another famous seaside holiday destination.

MAJOR ATTRACTIONS

Padmanabhaswamy temple

Padmanabhaswamy temple is located inside the East Fort. A fine blend of the Kerala and Dravidian styles of architecture, the temple has many legends associated with major narratives being dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The holy place is also known for its mural paintings and stone carvings. One among the 108 sacred Vishnu shrines in India, the presiding deity here is Lord Vishnu reclining on Anantha, the serpent.

As per the Hindu Holy Text 'Bhagwat Gita', Lord Balarama visited Padmanabhaswamy temple.

The first rocket that marked the beginning of the Indian Space Programme was launched from Thumba near Thiruvananthapuram .

Kovalam Beach

Kovalam is an internationally renowned beach, a favourite among tourists for decades. Life on the beach begins late in the day and carries on well into the night. The beach complex includes a string of budget cottages, Ayurvedic health resorts, convention and shopping zones, swimming pools, and yoga and Ayurvedic massage centres.

Vizhinjam Rock Cut Cave

To the south of Kovalam junction, the Vizhinjam Rock Cut Cave house sculptures dating back to the 18th century. The granite cave here encloses a one-celled shrine with a loose sculpture of Vinandhara Dakshinamurthi. The outer wall of the cave depicts half-complete relics of the Hindu God Shiva with Goddess Parvati.
